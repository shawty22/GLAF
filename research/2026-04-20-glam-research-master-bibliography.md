## TIER 1 — PRIMARY INSTITUTIONAL SOURCES
*Cite these first. Highest credibility for media, policy, and ESG audiences.*

### IEA — Energy and AI Report 2025
**Source:** International Energy Agency  
**URL:** https://www.iea.org/reports/energy-and-ai  
**Key data:**
- Global data center electricity consumption: **415 TWh in 2024** — doubling to **945 TWh by 2030**
- Growth rate: **15% per year** — more than four times faster than all other sectors combined
- AI-accelerated servers electricity consumption growing at **30% annually**
- US data centers expected to consume more electricity than all energy-intensive manufacturing combined by 2030 (aluminium, steel, cement, chemicals)
- Data centers will drive **almost half of US electricity demand growth** to 2030
- Renewable energy generation must grow by more than 450 TWh to meet data center demand by 2035

**Best for:** Opening stats in any presentation or pitch. The IEA is the gold standard for energy data.

---

### UNESCO / UCL — "Smarter, Smaller, Stronger" Report, July 2025
**Source:** UNESCO & University College London  
**URL:** https://www.unesco.org/en/articles/ai-large-language-models-new-report-shows-small-changes-can-reduce-energy-use-90  
**Key data:**
- Small, task-specific AI models can reduce energy consumption by **up to 90%** without loss of accuracy
- Each generative AI prompt uses approximately **0.34 watt-hours** of energy
- Global generative AI energy footprint already equivalent to **a low-income country's entire annual consumption**
- Three proven strategies: smaller task-specific models, shorter prompts (cut energy >50%), model quantization (saves up to 44%)
- "Mixture of experts" architecture activates only necessary modules — further reducing energy per query
- UNESCO: "Generative AI's annual energy footprint is already equivalent to that of a low-income country, and it is growing exponentially"
- UCL: "Too often, users rely on oversized AI models for simple tasks — it's like using a fire hose to water a house plant"

**Best for:** The UNESCO brand gives this unimpeachable credibility for climate and policy audiences. Use the 90% reduction figure prominently.

### Berkeley Lab / US DOE — US Data Center Energy Usage Report, December 2024
**Source:** Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory / US Department of Energy  
**URL:** https://eta-publications.lbl.gov/sites/default/files/2024-12/lbnl-2024-united-states-data-center-energy-usage-report.pdf  
**Key data:**
- AI-specific servers used **53–76 TWh** in the US in 2024
- Projected to reach **165–326 TWh by 2028**
- US data centers consumed approximately **17 billion gallons of water** for cooling in 2023
- Hyperscale facilities expected to consume **16–33 billion gallons annually by 2028**
- Data center electricity carbon intensity is **48% higher than the US average**

**Best for:** Water consumption statistics — your strongest environmental card. Completely undeniable.

### Stanford / Hazy Research — Intelligence Per Watt Study, November 2025
**Source:** Hazy Research, Stanford University  
**URL:** https://hazyresearch.stanford.edu/blog/2025-11-11-ipw  
**ArXiv:** https://arxiv.org/pdf/2511.07885  
**Key data:**
- Local models accurately handle **88.7% of single-turn chat and reasoning queries**
- Accuracy improved **3.1× from 2023–2025**
- Local intelligence efficiency improved **5.3× from 2023–2025** (3.1× model improvements + 1.7× hardware)
- Consumer laptops with 128GB unified memory can now run models approaching **200B parameters**
- Desktop GPUs enable **200B parameter models** with quantization
- Smartphone NPUs run **7B models at over 8 tokens/second**
- Local accelerator efficiency still has room to improve: Qwen3-32B on M4 Max shows 1.5× lower intelligence-per-watt vs NVIDIA B200 enterprise accelerator — but gap is narrowing fast

**Best for:** The technical credibility anchor. 1 million real-world queries tested. Peer-reviewed. Cite this for the "88.7%" and "5.3×" statistics.

### ArXiv — "Quantifying Energy and Cost Benefits of Hybrid Edge Cloud," January 2025
**Source:** Siavash Alamouti et al., ArXiv  
**URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/[search: Quantifying Energy Cost Benefits Hybrid Edge Cloud 2025]  
**Key data:**
- Hybrid local-edge AI vs pure cloud processing: energy savings **up to 75%**, cost reductions **exceeding 80%**
- Network and orchestration overhead accounts for **37–52% of total energy** in cloud AI requests — more than the inference itself
- "Edge processing directly utilises the local context to minimise computational complexity and avoids these cloud-scale energy demands"

**Best for:** The network overhead stat is devastating — more than half the energy of a cloud AI query is just moving data around, before the model even runs.

---

### ArXiv — "Small is Sufficient: Reducing World AI Energy Consumption Through Model Selection," October 2025
**Source:** ArXiv  
**URL:** https://arxiv.org/html/2510.01889v1  
**Key data:**
- Smart model selection (right-sizing queries to appropriate models) could save **16.25 TWh in the US in 2025 alone** — equivalent to two nuclear power reactors' annual output
- By 2028, these savings could reach **106 TWh globally** — equivalent to 5–17 nuclear reactors
- Three scenarios modelled: business as usual vs sobriety via model selection vs best-performing model on appropriate hardware

**Best for:** The nuclear reactor equivalency is viscerally compelling for climate audiences.

---

### MIT AI Infrastructure Sovereignty Paper, February 2026
**Source:** MIT (ArXiv)  
**URL:** https://arxiv.org/html/2602.10900v1  
**Key data:**
- Sovereignty increasingly depends on access to electricity, water, land, fibre infrastructure
- "Export controls, sanctions, and platform concentration have shown that access to compute, models, and infrastructure can be constrained by political decisions beyond the control of end users"
- As AI becomes critical to economic activity, public services, and national security, many regions are seeking sovereign AI that reduces exposure to unilateral disruption
- Existing discussions of sovereignty are insufficient — physical infrastructure control is the real dependency

**Best for:** The geopolitical and national security framing. Directly relevant to Steven's PSI work and IC contacts.

---

### MIT / ArXiv — "Securing Intelligence: Air-Gapped AI Systems," February 2026
**Source:** MIT dspace  
**URL:** https://dspace.mit.edu/handle/1721.1/164901  
**Key data:**
- Documents real-world data leakage: Samsung's proprietary source code leaked via ChatGPT — a landmark case
- Cloud-based API models pose significant risks including training data exposure and prompt injection attacks
- Data localization laws (GDPR etc.) underscore risks of outsourcing intelligence to cloud providers
- Air-gapped LLMs are the formal security recommendation for national security, defence, and intelligence contexts

**Best for:** The Samsung leak is a concrete, named incident that validates the security argument without being hypothetical.

### Brookings Institution — "Global Energy Demands Within the AI Regulatory Landscape," April 2026
**URL:** https://www.brookings.edu/articles/global-energy-demands-within-the-ai-regulatory-landscape/  
**Key data:**
- Google reduced data center emissions 12% through clean energy procurement — but absolute electricity consumption grew **27% year-over-year**
- IEA projects global data center consumption reaching **945 TWh by 2030, 1,200 TWh by 2035**
- China has announced 500+ data center infrastructure projects (2023–2024), 150+ running by end 2024
- "The efficiency paradox": efficiency gains slowing since 2020 while model size and query volume grow faster

**Best for:** The Google rebound effect stat. Demolishes the "we're going renewable" counterargument.

---

### World Economic Forum — "How Data Centres Can Avoid Doubling Their Energy Use by 2030," December 2025
**URL:** https://www.weforum.org/stories/2025/12/data-centres-and-energy-demand/

- Training ChatGPT generated an estimated **552 tonnes of CO2** — equivalent to annual footprint of **121 US households**
- Data centers consumed 415 TWh globally in 2024, doubling to 945 TWh by 2030 — equivalent to Japan's total annual electricity consumption

**Best for:** The ChatGPT training CO2 stat. Puts a face on the abstract numbers.

### ArXiv — "Energy Use of AI Inference: Efficiency Pathways and Test-Time Compute," September 2025
**URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.20241  
**Key data:**
- Non-production estimates and assumptions can overstate energy use by **4–20×** — most public estimates of cloud AI energy are based on assumptions, not measurements
- Results consistent with measurements using production-scale configurations

**Best for:** Epistemological humility — the actual cloud AI energy numbers may be even worse than reported, not better.

### McKinsey — "Sovereign AI: Ecosystems for Strategic Resilience," March 2026
**URL:** https://www.mckinsey.com/industries/technology-media-and-telecommunications/our-insights/sovereign-ai-building-ecosystems-for-strategic-resilience-and-impact

- Many sovereign AI initiatives are stalling — failing to translate intent into scaled adoption
- Sovereign AI refers to a nation's/organisation's ability to develop and control its own AI capabilities to ensure strategic independence and alignment with domestic values and laws
- Partnership models and ecosystem approach consistently outperform centralised sovereign AI programmes

**Best for:** Government and enterprise audiences. McKinsey's brand makes the sovereign AI argument credible to conservative institutional clients.

### Nextgov/FCW — "Microsoft Deploys Air-Gapped AI for Classified Defense," May 2024
**URL:** https://www.nextgov.com/artificial-intelligence/2024/05/microsoft-deploys-air-gapped-ai-classified-cloud/396354/  
**Key data:**
- Microsoft deployed GPT-4 for DoD classified networks — physically isolated from public internet
- Available via classified cloud-based systems but completely air-gapped from external networks
- DoD users can use generative AI for document drafting, code writing, and information analysis — but cannot train the model on new data

**Key data:**
- Purpose-built for federal desktops: large-scale AI workloads locally, sensitive data onsite
- Deterministic local inference capabilities ensure data stays secure in air-gapped or classified environments
- Optimised for geospatial data processing and secure on-premises LLM inference

---

### Tabnine — "What It Really Takes to Be Air-Gapped," June 2025
**URL:** https://www.tabnine.com/blog/what-it-really-takes-to-be-air-gapped/  
**Four non-negotiable criteria for true air-gapped AI:**
1. Zero external dependencies — no remote API calls, no cloud inference
2. Static, transparent model behaviour — weights inspectable, frozen, no silent updates
3. Local context processing only — no third-party embeddings or SaaS knowledge graphs
4. End-to-end auditability — every interaction fully traceable within host environment

**Key data:**
- Utility deals with tech giants often **raise residential electricity rates**
- Virginia study: ratepayers could pay an additional **$37.50 per month** to subsidise data centre energy costs
- Data centre growth is a **hidden tax on local communities** — disproportionately affecting lower-income areas near facilities

**Best for:** The social justice / environmental justice angle. Data centres extract value from communities. Local AI does not.

### Secure Privacy — "Data Privacy Trends 2026," December 2025
**URL:** https://secureprivacy.ai/blog/data-privacy-trends-2026  
**Key regulatory context:**
- **Gartner:** 75% of the world's population will operate under modern privacy regulation by end of 2024
- **GDPR:** 2,245 fines totalling €5.65 billion since 2018; 2025 alone: €2.3 billion (38% YoY increase)
- **EU Data Act** (effective September 2025): extends sovereignty beyond personal data to industrial and non-personal data; grants users rights to access and port information from connected devices; prohibits vendor lock-in
- **EU AI Act** fully applicable August 2, 2026 — risk-based obligations for high-impact systems
- 68% of privacy professionals now handle AI governance responsibilities

**Best for:** Regulatory urgency framing. The law is catching up to the problem local AI already solves structurally.
