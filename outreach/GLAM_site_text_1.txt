Manifesto

Artificial intelligence is the most significant infrastructure humanity has built this century. It is being produced behind closed doors, concentrated inside a handful of corporations, and deployed at planetary scale without any corresponding policy, agency, or common-law protections to govern it. The pattern is historically familiar. The response is too: constitutions, communities, and collective, distributed ownership have rebalanced concentrated power before. GLAM is the non-partisan, non-profit foundation doing that work for AI — around sovereignty, privacy, and safety — here.

01

Unplugging Skynet —
the real risk isn't a robot.

The Skynet we were warned about isn't a conscious machine deciding to destroy humanity. It's simpler than that — and it's already here. A handful of corporations now run the most consequential decision-making systems ever built, with no meaningful policy oversight and no off switch the public can reach. AI deciding what you can say. AI deciding whether you get a loan. AI deciding what counts as true — answerable to shareholders, not to constitutions or common law. Concentration is the amplifier of every other AI risk. One bad actor, one biased model, one corrupted update reaches everyone at once. Distribution is the structural fix. A world in which millions of communities run their own AI cannot be switched off, censored, or monopolised. A world where AI is open, locally owned, and ethically governed is structurally safer than a world where it isn't. GLAM is building that world — unplugging Skynet, one local model at a time.

02

Data sovereignty —
your intelligence stays yours.

Every query sent to a cloud AI becomes a data point owned by someone else. Questions, fears, plans, private thoughts — logged, stored, analysed, and monetised without meaningful consent. Local AI changes the architecture of ownership entirely. What runs on your machine belongs to you. What you ask stays with you. Sovereignty over one's own data is not a privacy setting. It is a right older than the cloud, with protections enshrined in most constitutions and in common law — and local AI is how people now exercise it.

03

Privacy —
not a feature. A right.

Privacy is enshrined in the Universal Declaration of Human Rights and in the common-law tradition that predates any platform. It is the condition that makes other freedoms — thought, expression, association — practically possible. When AI lives in someone else's cloud, privacy becomes a policy document most people never read, enforced by a corporation whose interests are not theirs. Local AI makes privacy structural rather than contractual. It is not a setting to switch on. It is the default state of intelligence that runs on hardware the user owns, answerable only to them.

04

Family safety —
AI that cannot be used against the people you love.

Children's questions. Family health searches. Household financial worries. Intimate conversations. These flow through corporate servers right now, building profiles that outlast the conversations themselves. Cloud AI develops a view of a family in ways never previously possible — and that view sits as an asset on someone else's balance sheet. Local AI removes that exposure at the source. What happens in the home stays in the home. It is not paranoia. It is a simple matter of agency over one's own information.

05

No corporate surveillance —
your mind is not a product.

The business model of cloud AI mirrors that of social media: attention and data are the product. Every question asked trains a model the asker does not own, builds a dataset they cannot see, and generates value that flows to shareholders they have never met. This is surveillance capitalism applied to the most intimate layer of human cognition — reasoning, curiosity, doubt. Local AI is how people exercise agency over that layer — not by asking permission, but by running something different.

06

Environmental conscience —
intelligence without a carbon bill.

US data centers consumed 183 terawatt-hours of electricity in 2024 — equal to the entire annual energy demand of Pakistan. They used 17 billion gallons of water for cooling. Natural gas and coal supply nearly 60% of that power. The communities living closest to these facilities — often the least wealthy, least responsible for emissions — bear the environmental cost of the world's AI queries. Local AI eliminates that infrastructure footprint at the source. No cooling towers. No transmission losses. No hidden carbon bill paid by someone else's community.

07

Ethical access —
the floor is rising.

Local AI models are getting more and more efficient. What required a $10,000 server in 2023 ran on a $600 Mac Mini by 2024. Intelligence-per-watt on local hardware improved 5.3x in two years. By 2027, a $500 device is on track to run AI that rivals today's frontier models. The trajectory is clear, it is accelerating, and it is ethical — bringing capable AI within reach of everyone, not only those who can already afford it. Which leads directly to the principle underneath all of it: open source.

08

Open source —
built by everyone, owned by no one.

The internet's core infrastructure is open source. Linux powers 96% of the world's servers. Wikipedia holds more human knowledge than any private encyclopaedia ever assembled. These are not accidents — they are evidence that the most durable, trustworthy, widely beneficial technologies are those no single entity owns. Open source behaves much like common law: built in public, improved by many, accountable to its users. GLAM builds on open models, contributes open databases, and shares open tools. AI that can be inspected, understood, and modified is AI that can be trusted. Closed models are promises. Open models are proof.

09

Global collaboration —
local everywhere, connected always.

GLAM means Global Local — and the tension in that name is intentional. Local AI is sovereign, private, and community-owned. The collaborative effort building it is planetary. A developer in Berlin, a climate scientist in Nairobi, an educator in São Paulo, and a community organiser in Detroit share the same tools, databases, and conviction: a better AI is possible. Intelligence runs locally. Collaboration runs globally. That is the working model for a technology that serves humanity rather than extracting from it.

10

The movement is
the product.

GLAM is not a startup. We are a non-profit, non-partisan foundation — not building an app, but building a collective of developers, scientists, educators, activists, and everyday people who collectively stay ahead of the frontier, build open tools, and ensure that local AI gets better, faster, and more accessible every year. Every new participant strengthens the agency of the whole. Every open tool makes the next one easier to build. Every community that adopts local AI makes the policy case more visible. The movement is the infrastructure. Join it.

11

Empowerment —
AI that makes you more human, not less.

Running underneath every principle in this manifesto is a single idea: that artificial intelligence should amplify human capability, dignity, and agency — not diminish them. AI that surveils people diminishes them. AI that extracts their data diminishes them. AI owned by actors whose interests are not theirs diminishes them. Local AI — private, open, community-owned, environmentally honest, ethically governed — puts the person at the centre. Not as a user. Not as a data point. As a human being with sovereignty over their own intelligence. That is what empowerment means. That is what GLAM is building.

"We are not against artificial intelligence. We are against artificial intelligence that serves the few at the cost of the many. We are building the alternative — one local model, one open database, one community at a time. Join us. The future of AI is local. And it belongs to everyone."

The environmental cost of cloud AI

Every query.

Every day.

Someone pays.

ChatGPT processes 2.5 billion queries every single day. US data centers consumed 183 terawatt-hours of electricity in 2024 — equivalent to the entire nation of Pakistan. They used 17 billion gallons of water for cooling. Natural gas and coal supply nearly 60% of that power. That number doubles by 2030.

Most people using AI every day have no idea this is happening. The cloud is invisible. The cost is paid by the environment, by communities near data centers, and by a planet that cannot afford it. Local AI eliminates that footprint entirely — and the research proves it works at scale.
